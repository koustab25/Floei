The luteal phase begins after ovulation. Progesterone rises to prepare the uterus for potential pregnancy, then falls if no pregnancy occurs.

What's happening in your body?

  • Progesterone dominates this phase
  • Basal body temperature remains elevated
  • PMS symptoms may appear in late phase
  • Energy levels gradually decrease

Self-Care Strategies

Gentle Movement

Yoga, walking, and stretching help manage stress and bloating.

Prioritize Sleep

Progesterone makes you sleepier - honor your need for extra rest.

Organize & Plan

Use your natural attention to detail for administrative tasks.

Reflect & Review

Your introspective energy is perfect for journaling and evaluation.

PMS Nutrition

Mood Support

  • Complex carbohydrates
  • Dark leafy greens
  • Omega-3 rich foods

Bloating Relief

  • Celery & cucumber
  • Potassium-rich bananas
  • Herbal diuretics like dandelion tea

Serotonin Boosters

  • Dark chocolate
  • Chickpeas & lentils
  • Pumpkin seeds
